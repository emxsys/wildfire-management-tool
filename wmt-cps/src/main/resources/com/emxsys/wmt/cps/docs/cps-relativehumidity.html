Relative Humidity

"Saturation of surface air is a condition of favorable fire weather; that is, conductive to low fire danger. Less favorable are conditions of unsaturation that permit evaporation from forest fuels, increasing their flammability and the fire danger. Relative humidity (RH) is the measure of atmospheric moisture in a volume of air to the total amount which that volume can hold at the given temperature and atmospheric pressure. Relative humidity is the ratio of actual vapor pressure to saturation to near zero for very dry air. Relative humidity depends on the actual moisture content of the air, the temperature, and the pressure."

Reference page 37 of the Fire Weather Book, NFE 1174.
